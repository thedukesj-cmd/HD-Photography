The entire Aperture Club community joins together to congratulate **Elena Vasquez** on an extraordinary achievement: a 12-page portfolio feature in the **March 2024 issue of National Geographic** magazine.

## The Portfolio: "Cathedrals of Ice"

Elena's feature, titled *Cathedrals of Ice*, documents the dramatic transformation of alpine ecosystems across the Swiss, Austrian, and Italian Alps over a three-year period. The work is both strikingly beautiful and deeply urgent — a visual record of landscapes being fundamentally altered by climate change.

> "I wanted to make images that were undeniably gorgeous, so that people would lean in — and then feel the weight of what they were actually looking at. Beauty as a Trojan horse for environmental truth."
> — Elena Vasquez

## Three Years in the Making

Elena began this project in 2021, making four extended expeditions per year to document the same locations across all seasons. The resulting 8,000+ image archive was edited over six months with the National Geographic picture desk before the final 24 images were selected for the print feature.

## A Personal Note from Elena

*"I am deeply grateful for the Aperture Club community. The early feedback I received when I presented the beginnings of this project at our monthly meeting in 2021 helped shape the direction of the work in ways I'm still discovering. This recognition belongs, in part, to all of you."*

The March 2024 issue of National Geographic is available now in print and digitally. Elena will be presenting the full project — including images not published in the magazine — at our **April club meeting**. Details to follow.

Congratulations, Elena. We couldn't be more proud.
